Geo political aspect of information security

Dec 22, 2015
2 minute read

Any kind of IT operation could have some kind of potential threat. We can’t assume a source of information that is free of risks towards such threats. This kind of risk rises enormously in case of outsourcing or

offshoring. If we look into the data more then 60% of breach of actions were caused after the involvement of a third party. In case of outsourcing, if the third party organization is not directly liable for safety and security of the assets of the organization or its customers, then there is a much greater possibility of rise in the threats that the organization posses. Is there any way this can be guaranteed? This is the biggest question.

Since, the liability could be the major point of failure in case of risk associated with the outsourcing threat, it is important that the correct policies and regulations should be applied to make the outsourced organization liable for any breach of security.

There are many ways we can manage risk, but the most important one in case of outsourcing or offshoring is risk transference. Risk transference can be done by following an appropriate information security governance and transfer of associated liability. This means If an organization A assigns a certain part of it task say X to organization B, then the risk associated with task X should be directly transferred to organization B. A real time example could be transfer of risk to the payment service provider by using the industry standard regulation such as PCI DSS. By doing so the merchant could make the payment service provider liable for the information at its disposal. Besides PCI DSS, there are many other standard frameworks like ISO 2700 series, SOX, HIPPA etc. These could be either state standard or industry standard.

Having said that, organization should also enforce appropriate security policy, sign an NDA and perform security audit against the third party so as to secure the assets or the transactions associated with the business functionalities. This causes the policy to be enforced not only to the direct employees but also to inside-out employees (meaning the employees outside the organization who can access the company’s data).

Despite being a potential cyber security threat, outsourcing or offshoring have been really important aspects in the IT industry by helping organizations use their fields of expertise and thus increasing the industry efficiency, resulting in the financial benefit.

Conclusion? We can’t live without outsourcing - that is a fact. So, what is it that goes wrong here? Outsourcing is not itself a threat to security, but a bad outsourcing decision is in most of the cases. One fundamental stuff that organizations should consider is to put security as an important aspect during the procurement process.
