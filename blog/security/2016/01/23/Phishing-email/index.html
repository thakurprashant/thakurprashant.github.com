Phishing email

Jan 23, 2016
1 minute read

Phishing emails are composed in such a way that the information in the email attracts a recipient to click on a malicious link or download a malicious content. Most of the times these emails are random and we don’t care much about. However, at times we have attackers targeting a victim with sufficient research on the subject matter the possible victim might be interested in. Say, if you are traveling to some place, you might be sent a phishing email with offers on hotels for your stay. These types of phishing emails are known as spear-phishing.

In general there are few things that can keep you safe from phising email attacks. Some of these are:

  1. Always remember that the email address of the sender could be fake and can’t be trusted.

  2. Links may be spoofed, so copy the link and paste on the browser’s address bar to check if it is genuine.

  3. Attachments should only be clicked or downloaded if its something you are expecting from someone you already know or trust.

  4. Check for spellings and grammars. Any mistake as such is always suspicious.

  5. Unclear salutations or salutations without your name should not be trusted.

  6. Check the signatures, most of the times phishing emails do not have genuine signatures but this is not always the case. Sometimes fake signatures might seem legitimate.

  7. If possible check the source. This could the best thing to be done for final confirmation.
